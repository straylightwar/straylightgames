Materials Needed to Play

  • 1. Dice - Three colors of dice are required to play. Ideally, Blue, Red, and Black dice, but in theory, any three colors will do. Players should have about 6-10 of each color.
  • 2. Player Tokens - These represent the players on the board. These should be opposing colors, or team colors if team play. Miniatures can also be used.
  • 3. Maps - The surface that players battle on is called a map, they are easily printed from the maps page. New maps can easily be made with the map template.
  • 4. Modifier Cards - High resolution images of the modifier cards are located in the modifications section of the page. These cards change how the game plays, and are available in 300dpi resolution designed for print and play.
  • 5. Game Documentation - Having the Game Documentation around makes it easier to reference the game rules. Because it's available in a digital format, it's portable, and has a dice simulator if you're missing dice!

Dice Terminology

  • Amount - This is the amount of physical dice.
  • Total - This is the sum of the dice rolled
  • Roll Against/For - When dice are rolled against each other, they are reordered highest to lowest values, then compared individually to see which dice succeed or fail.
  • Success and Failure - A successful roll exceeds the other dice roll. Defensive rolls are considered successful if they roll an equal value to their opposition.
