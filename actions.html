Turn Sequence

Any beginning of turn map specific events take place, such as spawning characters.

Next the beginning of each turn, all players roll their speed (blue) dice and total their values to see who moves first.

Ties are broken by rolling speed (blue) dice again.

Characters may opt to move and take actions later than the order they rolled, but not earlier.

Characters then move and take actions, interact with obstacles, and resolve combat in any or mixed order.

Any end of turn map sequences take place, such as refreshing objects.

Actions

Melee Combat

When two or more units are adjacent, they may choose to block one another's movement and engage in melee combat with the other-- this cannot be prevented except by defeating the other player(s) or by modification cards.

An attacking player chooses a target and rolls their offense (red) dice, ordering them from highest to lowest

The defending player then rolls their defense (black) dice, ordering them from highest to lowest.

The reordered dice are then compared, attacks rolled higher than defense dice are successful and defense rolled equal or higher than attacks are successful.

If the amount of attack dice is higher than the amount of defense dice, they must roll 3 or higher to be successful.

Multiple characters can also be targeted by splitting the amount of dice rolled between each target, or by modification cards.

Ranged Combat

Attacking players at a distance is accomplished the same way as melee combat except for a few differences for the attacker.

Ranged attacks must have an unobstructed path to the target, unless otherwise modified.

Range is determined by the amount of attack dice for each tile beyond melee range, and that die is subtracted from the attack roll.

For example, a character with 5 offense (red) dice, can attack up to 4 tiles away, using the last die to attack with.

Multiple characters can also be targeted by splitting the amount of dice rolled between each target, or by modification cards.

Damage

Damage from successful hits is subtracted from a player's dice pool (Speed, Offense, or Defense).

The set subtracted from is the damaged player's choice, unless modified otherwise.

A player must maintain at least 1 die in each pool, or they are injured and disabled.

If a character loses all of their dice in their dice pool, they are destroyed.

Advanced Play: Instead of being injured, dice pools with zero in them can just disable that specific trait (Speed, Offense, or Defense) until the player is destroyed.
